Abstract

Sleep is a weird phenomena that occurs in most earth animals, that involves spending a large amount of time laying in place doing absolutely nothing. How long an animal sleeps depends mostly on species, but also on how old it is.

Humans are obsessed with time. All humans carry time keeping devices known as clocks, and will schedule events relative to their state.

Humans have little control over how long they sleep for, so they have invented clocks that set of alarms at specific times, known as alarm clocks.

The alarm will force the human out of the state of sleep, which they find extremely unpleasant.

Beds

Most humans own at least one sleeping station. These are contained in dedicated sleeping rooms.

The bed is by far the most popular sleeping device. In the image on the right you can see a bed in its natural habitat- the bed room.

Sleeping devices do not have to be stationary, there are both psuedo-portable and fully portable options.

Generally speaking, the more portable a sleeping station is, the less comfortable.

Dreams

Most animals capable of cognition have dreams while in their sleeping state. Dreams are superficial recreations of the world around them, but as their brains are partially off they are seldom able to detect this.

If they do, they enter what is called a lucid dream, which is that they are able to control and shape the dream world around them because they are aware of the fact that they are dreaming.

Sheep

A popular activity amongst humans is to count sheep. Sometimes they use real sheep, and some times they simply imagine them. Reportedly this activity helps the humans fall asleep.
